Mercimek

Mercimek Çorbası is a classic Turkish lentil soup made with red lentils, onions, and carrots, seasoned with cumin and paprika. It’s warm, hearty, and perfect for a comforting meal. A squeeze of lemon and red pepper flakes add extra flavor, making it a beloved staple in Turkish cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups red lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cumin (optional)
  • 2 liters hot water
  • 5 tablespoons vegetable oil

For the sauce

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground red pepper

Steps

  1. To make red lentil soup, heat vegetable oil in a pot and sauté chopped onions until they turn light pink.
  2. Then, add flour and continue to sauté over low heat.
  3. If using tomato paste, add it and sauté briefly. Then, add diced carrots and thoroughly washed and drained red lentils.
  4. Pour in water, stir, and cover the pot. Cook over medium heat until it boils, then reduce the heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the lentils and carrots are tender.
  5. Once cooked, blend the soup with a hand blender until smooth. If you don’t have a blender, you can pass it through a sieve.
  6. Add black pepper, salt, and optionally cumin, then stir well. Cook for another 5 minutes before removing from heat.
  7. Meanwhile, melt two tablespoons of butter in a small pan, add one teaspoon of ground red pepper, and remove from heat.

Pour the red pepper butter over the soup when serving. Serve with a slice of lemon and enjoy!
